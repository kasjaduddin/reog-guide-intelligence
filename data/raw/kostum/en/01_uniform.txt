1. Costume of King Klono Sewandono
King Klono Sewandono is depicted as a powerful ruler who possesses the sacred whip, Pecut Samandiman. His gallant and noble demeanor is reflected through agile and dignified dance movements. According to the legend, he created this beautiful art to fulfill his lover’s request, so his dance also occasionally expresses a romantic, lovestruck emotion. The dancer portraying this character wears a red mask adorned with various decorative patterns.
Properties used by King Klono Sewandono include:
 Red cinde trousers, jarit (batik cloth), boro-boro, samir, stagen (waistband), epek timang (belt buckle), sampur (shawl), uncal, kace (necklace cloth), kalung (necklace), cakep, klat, nahu, probo, keris (dagger), and binggel (bracelets).

2. Jathil
Jathil represents a young soldier in training, performing while riding a horse. The dancer uses a kuda kepang — a woven bamboo hobbyhorse painted to resemble a real horse. The Jathil’s character combines agility, firmness, and at times graceful movements.
Costume and properties used by Jathil include:
 Bordered panji trousers, white shirt, jarit parang srimpi, boro-boro and samir, stagen, sabuk epek timang (belt), red and yellow sampur (shawls), kace, srempang, cakep, binggel (bracelets), udeng (headcloth), and eblek (woven horse).

3. Warok
The Warok character appears in Reog performances as a warrior and guardian. The Warok dancer is typically portrayed with a red-painted face, thick beard, and dressed entirely in black attire called penadon, accompanied by a white or red waistband known as kolor or usus-usus. In Reog performances, Warok figures serve as the guards (punggawa) of King Klono Sewandono, consisting of both young and elder Warok.
Young Warok are depicted as strong men training in martial arts, with muscular bodies, hairy chests, thick mustaches, and sharp eyes. Meanwhile, the elder Warok are shown as mentors or overseers, characterized by a thin body, long white beard, and a walking stick.
Properties used by Warok include:
 Black kombor trousers, black shirt, jarit, stagen, epek timang, kolor, udeng, jinjen and tapak doro, debleng mondholan, waktung, keris (dagger), and long-sleeved white shirt.

4. Pembarong (Singo Barong)
Singo Barong is the dancer character with the head of a Javanese tiger decorated with peacock feathers. The performer moves by swinging the tiger head back and forth, rolling on the ground, and lowering it dramatically. This character serves as the most iconic and symbolic figure of Reog Ponorogo, wearing a massive mask called Dadak Merak.
The property used by Singo Barong is a large lion head mask adorned with peacock feathers, reaching a height of about 1–2 meters above the head.

5. Patih Bujangganong
Bujangganong is one of the principal Reog dancers, portraying the young prime minister (patih) of King Klono Sewandono. He is known for being agile, clever, humorous, and powerful. The dancer’s costume is usually open and dominated by red or black, often embellished with fringes or knitted ornaments in bright red or yellow.
Properties used by Bujangganong include:
 A red mask, red vest, trousers, embong gombyog (tassels), stagen, epek timang, red and yellow sampur (shawls), red vest with black stripes, and binggel (bracelets).
