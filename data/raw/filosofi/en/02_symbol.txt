SYMBOLS OF REOG PONOROGO:
1. Kelana Sewandono
Kelana Sewandana is depicted as a king. He has a gallant appearance. The symbol of Kelana Sewandana is interpreted as a hero who saved or raised Ponorogo. This character is a role model for the community. The mask used by the character Kelana Sewandono has a red face, a king's crown, a delimo mletek mouth shape, dondongan eyes, and kuwel eyebrows. This mask symbolizes courage, wisdom, and authority. This character is depicted as a savior or hero. King Kelana Sewandono's clothing is predominantly red and golden yellow. These colors symbolize the authority and wisdom possessed by a king.
2. Samandiman Whip
This whip or whip is the main weapon used by King Kelana Sewandhana. This whip is used to fight against Singo Barong when they meet in the forest. The whip is a very powerful weapon, causing the king who rules the forest, SingobBarong, to kneel. The Samandiman whip is the whip used by King Kelana Sewandhana. This whip has decorations resembling small circles in yellow and red, made from several strands of wool thread woven together. There are seven of these decorations. The number seven is interpreted as the human senses that must always be guarded in order to receive blessings from the Almighty. This means that a leader must have a “hold” or ultimate weapon.
3. The character Bujang Ganong
Bujang Ganong is one of the characters in the Reog performance whose appearance is eagerly awaited by the audience. Although this character appears comical, he possesses extraordinary powers. This has given rise to a myth in society about human nature. Based on the bantarangin version of the story, Bujang Ganong or Patih Pujanggo Anom was the prime minister of King Kenana Sewandana who was sent to propose to Dyah Ayu Dewi Songgolangit, the princess of the Kediri kingdom. This character is a companion and guardian as well as a comical character in the performance. The character of Bujang Ganong depicts a young prime minister who is agile, clever, humorous, and powerful.
4. Dhadak Merak and Singo Barong
Dhadak Merak is the main element in the Reog Ponorogo performance. This element consists of a tiger head made of dadap wood, bamboo, and rattan covered with tiger skin. Dhadak Merak is used as a place to arrange peacock feathers. Dhadak Merak looks beautiful when it spreads its feathers and when it bites the tasbih (a means of remembrance or reminder of God). The meaning contained in Dhahak Merak with the combination of beautiful peacock feathers is strength, beauty, power, and courage. This character is central to the story and is the main character. These two contrasting animals are embodied in a mask that symbolizes coexistence and peace. The hope is that the people of Ponorogo will have a polite, courageous, and authoritative character and always bring peace. Meanwhile, Singo Barong is shaped like a tiger's head with a human body, topped with a peacock. Based on the teachings of Triloka, this character has a meaning or interpretation, namely that the peacock represents Alam Niskala/svarloka (the world of the gods), the tiger head represents Alam Sakala Niskala/bhuvarloka (the world of the purified), while the person wearing this mask represents Alam Sakala/Bhurloka (the world of humans). The meaning is that as humans, if we want to achieve perfection in life, we must go through various trials.
